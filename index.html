As a Mellon May Fellow, I intend on studying a Mexican traditional dance known as Baile Folklorico to explore how the intersections of sexuality, gender, and citizenship create a more unified Mexican community. There is a history with the Mexican community and the LGBTQIA community that is brushed off because approaching the matter is difficult for most. Because of this, many issues have arisen from peopleâ€™s misunderstanding of how the intersections go hand in hand with Baile Folklorico. The dance is rooted in many old-fashioned traditional beliefs where discrimination begins to take place. The issue here is that other communities continue to grow while some of the Mexican community are still advocating for anti-gay or anti-black beliefs. As someone who identifies as LatinX and of Mexican descent, I see a huge issue within the Mexican community that revolves around speaking up or saying what is right. This project's contribution to scholarly audiences and a wider public lies in its analysis of how and why Baile Folklorico encourages those who identify as Mexican to understand how and why baile folklore intersects with sexuality, gender, and citizenship so that unity is spread throughout the community. Issues revolving around homophobia, transphobia, and xenophobia are still relevant today and happen every day. Focusing on the Mexican community, it is important that we understand as a whole that no matter what background, there needs to be a sense of togetherness, family. It is also important that those who do not identify as Mexican are able to understand the intersections and relate them to their own experiences, allowing room for a much more positive oral tradition in Mexican communities. This research topic could lead to a greater understanding of new and evolving Mexican traditions that allow room for other identities, races, and ethnicities to be viewed as equal within the community.
